About.

Destiny & Hope Foundation is fully independent of any political party. We rely on donations and help from our thousands of supporters to effect the change we want to see.

Company.

In a World faced with severe climate change and recurring financial crises it is clear the current economic system is not fit for purpose. A need exists for radical transition to a new economics in Africa, one that can deliver for its people and for the planet. We observe with great trepidation how most of the industrialised economies are increasingly unsustainable, unfair and unstable; meanwhile the African continent is still seen as the Dark Continent faced by grinding poverty, disease and death. After decades of fruitless and debilitating aid we are now faced with donor fatigue and increasingly the continent is being left to its own devices. The recent surge in mass emigration by Africans, willing to risk their lives in treacherous waters to escape the misery that is their existence, calls for urgent revaluation of our political, economic and social life. The need for African countries to relook their strategies and to chart a new future growth path is now if we are to improve the wellbeing of their people and that of future generations.

Mission.

Our mission is to kick-start the move to a new economy through big ideas and fresh thinking. We aim to do this through:

  1. Training & development of independent young people in economics, business and entrepreneurship
  2. High quality, ground-breaking research that shows what is wrong with the current economy and how it can be better
  3. Demonstrating the power of our ideas by putting them into action in the projects we undertake
  4. Working with other organisations locally, regionally and across the world, to build a movement for economic change

Advisory Council


The Advisory Council for the Destiny & Hope Foundation consists of thoughtful leaders who are instrumental in ensuring the longevity of our organization by providing advice and guidance to the Board of Directors. The commitment of the Advisory Council is also to raise sufficient funding for the operation of the foundation annually.

The Advisory Council also serves to:


  1. Advise the Board of Directors on the fundraising strategy and contribute their expertise and thinking to the current and future work of the Foundation.
  2. Attend at least two Board of Directors meetings per year (including the Annual Report meeting held in February)

We recognize the services our Advisory Council by:


  1. Naming a scholarship in their honor (or name of their choice)
  2. Inviting them to an exclusive end of year event every year
  3. Acting on their advice and guidance
